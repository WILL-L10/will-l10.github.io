Major Projects

1. Complete Home Electrical Rewiring

Location: Las Vegas, NV
Duration: 3 months
Role: Lead Electrician Assistant

Project Scope

Completely rewired a 2,400 sq ft residence built in 1975, bringing it up to current NEC (National Electrical Code) standards while preserving the home’s structure.

Key Accomplishments:

  • 200A Service Panel Upgrade

    • Replaced outdated 100A fuse box with modern 200A circuit breaker panel
    • Installed 40 circuit positions with AFCI/GFCI protection
    • Coordinated with utility company for temporary disconnect
    • Load balancing across phases for optimal power distribution

  • Complete Branch Circuit Replacement

    • Removed all knob-and-tube wiring (1,200+ linear feet)
    • Installed new 12/2 and 14/2 Romex throughout
    • 45+ new circuit runs for lighting, outlets, and appliances
    • Strategic placement to minimize visible conduit

  • Modern Safety Features

    • GFCI protection in all wet areas (kitchen, bathrooms, garage)
    • AFCI protection in bedrooms and living areas
    • Grounded outlets throughout (replaced 100+ ungrounded)
    • Dedicated circuits for major appliances

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Hidden Knob-and-Tube Wiring

Problem: Discovered active knob-and-tube in walls during demolition, requiring expansion of project scope.

Solution:

  • Systematically traced all circuits using tone generator
  • Created detailed map of existing wiring before removal
  • Coordinated with homeowner for additional wall access
  • Completed removal with minimal additional drywall damage

Challenge 2: Limited Attic Access

Problem: 18-inch high attic with insulation made wire routing difficult.

Solution:

  • Used flexible drill bits for blind drilling between joists
  • Employed fish tape and pull strings for wire routing
  • Worked during cooler morning hours (attic reached 130°F)
  • Installed junction boxes at strategic access points

Challenge 3: Grounding in Older Construction

Problem: No existing grounding system; metal water pipes replaced with PVC.

Solution:

  • Installed 8-foot copper ground rods at service entrance
  • Bonded to water main before PVC transition
  • Ran #6 copper to all subpanels
  • Verified ground resistance < 25Ω with megger

2. Oak Hardwood Floor Installation

Location: Multiple residential properties, Las Vegas
Total Area: 8,000+ sq ft across 12+ projects
Role: Lead Installer

Project Highlights

Materials Expertise:

  • 3/4" solid red oak (most common)
  • 5/8" engineered hickory
  • 1/2" engineered maple
  • Various stain colors and finishes

Installation Techniques:

  1. Subfloor Preparation
   Quality Checklist:
   ✓ Moisture content < 12% (moisture meter)
   ✓ Flatness within 1/8" per 10 feet
   ✓ Squeaks eliminated (screws every 6")
   ✓ 15-lb felt paper underlayment
   ✓ Proper expansion gap (3/4" perimeter)

  1. Layout & Planning

    • Calculate square footage + 10% waste factor
    • Determine board direction (perpendicular to joists)
    • Plan starting point for visual appeal
    • Identify obstacle locations (vents, doorways)

  2. Installation Process

   Step-by-Step Method:
   1. Snap chalk lines for starter row
   2. Face-nail first row (predrill to prevent splits)
   3. Blind-nail at 45° angle with pneumatic nailer
   4. Maintain 1/8" gap between boards (racking)
   5. Use rubber mallet for tight seams
   6. Stagger end joints minimum 6"
   7. Cut around obstacles with jigsaw
   8. Install transition strips at doorways
  1. Finishing
    • Drum sanding (80, 100, 120 grit progression)
    • Edge sanding for perimeter
    • Hand scraping in corners
    • 3 coats polyurethane (oil-based)
    • Light sanding between coats (220 grit)

Notable Project: 2,400 sq ft Two-Story Home

Scope: Complete main floor and upstairs hallway

Specifications:

  • Material: 3/4" solid red oak, 2.25" wide
  • Pattern: Straight lay (perpendicular to floor joists)
  • Finish: Dark walnut stain + satin polyurethane
  • Timeline: 2 weeks (installation + finishing)

Challenges:

1. Uneven Subfloor

  • Found 1/2" dip in living room
  • Solution: Installed tapered shims under felt paper
  • Verified flatness with 6-foot straightedge

2. HVAC Vent Integration

  • 6 floor vents requiring precise cutouts
  • Solution: Created cardboard templates
  • Used router with flush-trim bit for clean edges
  • Custom fabricated oak vent covers

3. Staircase Transition

  • Complex geometry at staircase landing
  • Solution: Scribed boards to fit stair nose
  • Installed hardwood stair treads to match floor
  • Used quarter-round for seamless transition

Tools & Equipment

Power Tools:

  • Pneumatic flooring nailer (18-gauge)
  • Compressor (6-gallon pancake)
  • Miter saw (12" sliding compound)
  • Table saw (for rip cuts)
  • Drum sander (commercial grade)
  • Edge sander
  • Pneumatic brad nailer (trim work)

Hand Tools:

  • Rubber mallet (for seating boards)
  • Pull bar (for last row installation)
  • Tap block (protects tongue during nailing)
  • Moisture meter (verify wood acclimation)
  • Chalk line
  • Measuring tape & speed square
  • Pry bar & hammer

Quality Standards

Acceptance Criteria:

  • ✅ No visible gaps between boards
  • ✅ Smooth, level surface (no lips between boards)
  • ✅ Consistent nail depth (flush, not overdriven)
  • ✅ Proper expansion gap at walls (covered by baseboard)
  • ✅ Uniform stain color
  • ✅ Smooth finish (no bubbles, runs, or rough spots)

3. Complete Bathroom Renovation

Location: Las Vegas, NV
Duration: 6 weeks
Role: General Contractor / Lead Installer

Project Scope

Transformed dated 1980s bathroom into modern, functional space including demolition, plumbing upgrades, tile work, and electrical.

Before Specifications:

  • Single vanity with laminate countertop
  • Fiberglass tub/shower combo
  • Vinyl flooring
  • Builder-grade lighting
  • Limited storage

After Specifications:

  • Double vanity with quartz countertop
  • Custom tile shower with glass enclosure
  • Porcelain tile flooring (12"x24")
  • Recessed lighting + vanity lighting
  • Built-in storage niches

Phase 1: Demolition & Rough-In

Demolition Process:

Day 1-2: Complete Tearout
├─ Remove vanity, toilet, fixtures
├─ Demo fiberglass tub/shower
├─ Remove vinyl flooring to subfloor
├─ Strip drywall to studs
└─ Expose plumbing and electrical

Day 3-4: Rough-In Work
├─ Relocate drain lines for double vanity
├─ Install new vent stack
├─ Run new water lines (PEX)
├─ Add electrical circuits
│  ├─ Dedicated 20A GFCI for outlets
│  └─ Separate 15A for lighting
└─ Install exhaust fan duct

Plumbing Upgrades:

  • Replaced galvanized supply lines with PEX
  • Installed shutoff valves at all fixtures
  • Moved drain locations for new vanity layout
  • Added shower valve with thermostatic control
  • Installed linear drain in shower floor

Electrical Upgrades:

  • 20A GFCI circuit for vanity outlets
  • 15A circuit for recessed lighting (4x LED)
  • Dedicated fan/light combo circuit
  • Heated mirror defogger
  • All wiring in conduit where exposed

Phase 2: Waterproofing & Tile

Shower Waterproofing System:

Layer Stack (inside to outside):
1. Cement board (1/2" HardieBacker)
2. RedGard liquid membrane (2 coats)
3. Kerdi waterproofing fabric at seams
4. Thinset mortar bed
5. Porcelain tile
6. Grout + sealer

Tile Installation Specs:

Installation Process:

  1. Layout Planning

    • Drew full-scale plan on paper
    • Calculated tile requirements + 15% waste
    • Identified focal points (shower entrance, vanity wall)
    • Planned symmetrical layout

  2. Substrate Preparation

    • Installed cement board with thinset
    • Screwed every 6" into studs
    • Taped all seams with fiberglass mesh
    • Applied liquid waterproofing membrane

  3. Tile Installation

    • Mixed thinset to peanut butter consistency
    • Used 1/4" square-notch trowel
    • Set tiles with 1/8" spacers
    • Checked level every 3 rows
    • Back-buttered all tiles for 100% coverage

  4. Grouting

    • Waited 48 hours for thinset to cure
    • Mixed sanded grout (4:1 powder:water ratio)
    • Applied with rubber float at 45° angle
    • Removed haze with damp sponge
    • Applied sealer after 72-hour cure

Phase 3: Finish Work

Vanity Installation:

  • Assembled 72" double-sink vanity
  • Leveled and secured to wall studs
  • Installed quartz countertop (fabricated offsite)
  • Mounted undermount sinks
  • Connected plumbing (P-traps, supply lines)
  • Installed faucets with deck-mount valves

Glass Shower Enclosure:

  • Measured opening after tile completion
  • Ordered custom frameless glass (3/8" tempered)
  • Installed U-channel at curb
  • Hung door with continuous hinge
  • Applied silicone sealant at all joints

Lighting & Ventilation:

  • Installed 4× 6" LED recessed lights
  • Mounted dual vanity lights (36" each)
  • Installed 110 CFM exhaust fan
  • Added dimmer switches for ambiance

Skills Demonstrated

✅ Project Management: Coordinated multiple trades (plumber, electrician, tile setter)
✅ Waterproofing: Critical for preventing water damage
✅ Tile Layout: Aesthetic planning and precision cutting
✅ Finish Carpentry: Trim work and detail-oriented installation

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Shower Floor Slope

Problem: Floor needed 1/4" per foot slope to linear drain.

Solution:

  • Built mortar bed with proper slope using screed guides
  • Used level and straightedge to verify pitch
  • Allowed 48-hour cure before waterproofing
  • Result: Perfect drainage with no standing water

Challenge 2: Out-of-Square Walls

Problem: Walls were out of square by 3/8" over 8 feet.

Solution:

  • Adjusted tile layout to hide discrepancies
  • Started from center and worked outward
  • Used wider grout lines on one side to compensate
  • Result: Visually square appearance despite structural issues

Challenge 3: Vanity Plumbing Conflict

Problem: Drain location didn’t align with vanity.

Solution:

  • Cut access panel in back of vanity
  • Offset drain with 45° fittings
  • Verified code compliance (proper venting)
  • Result: Clean installation with hidden modifications
